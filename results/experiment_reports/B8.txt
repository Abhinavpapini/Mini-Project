=====================================================================
WHY ICA FAILS
=====================================================================

1. ICA MAXIMISES INDEPENDENCE, NOT DISCRIMINABILITY:
   FastICA finds statistically independent components (maximises non-Gaussianity
   via logcosh contrast function). These independent sources may correspond to
   distinct acoustic generators (speaker tract, noise, background), but they
   do NOT correspond to stutter-type dimensions — stutter events are products
   of multiple simultaneous acoustic factors (articulatory + respiratory).

2. CATASTROPHICALLY LOW RECALL (Macro-Rec=0.203):
   With precision=0.575 but recall=0.203, the model is extremely conservative —
   it only predicts positive when absolutely certain. This suggests the ICA-32
   components produce very uneven feature distributions per class, making
   threshold calibration very difficult.

3. PROLONGATION COLLAPSE (F1=0.102, Recall=0.056):
   Prolongation's acoustic signature (vowel elongation) spans multiple ICA
   components simultaneously. No single component or combination is predictive,
   leading to near-total failure. Recall=5.6% means almost all Prolongation
   clips are missed.

4. ICA SOLUTION IS NOT UNIQUE (sign/order ambiguity):
   FastICA components have sign ambiguity (±) and are unordered. This makes
   the 32 components harder for the CNN to interpret than PCA's ordered,
   sign-consistent components sorted by explained variance.

5. TRAINING NEVER CONVERGES (F1 oscillates 0.03-0.28 over 30 epochs):
   The ICA feature space is not smooth enough for the CNN to find stable
   decision boundaries. The model finds no consistent direction of improvement.
