=====================================================================
ANALYSIS
=====================================================================

1. PERTURBATION CREATES TRAIN/TEST DISTRIBUTION MISMATCH:
   The model trains on noisy features but tests on clean features.
   The train_loss curve is consistently higher than val_loss (different
   distributions), which desynchronises gradient descent. The model
   learns to be very conservative (high precision, low recall) to avoid
   false positives on noisy training samples that don't appear at test time.

2. HUBERT FEATURES ARE ALREADY NOISE-ROBUST:
   HuBERT is trained with masked prediction — it already learns robust
   representations that ignore random perturbations. Adding jitter to
   PCA-compressed HuBERT features adds noise that wasn't in the original
   SSL pre-training distribution. The model correctly ignores this noise
   but at the cost of overall sensitivity.

3. RECALL COLLAPSE (0.362) MORE SEVERE THAN F2:
   F2 (mixup): recall=0.401 | F5 (perturbation): recall=0.362
   The combined perturbations (jitter + scale + dropout) destroy more
   discriminative signal than mixup alone, making it harder for the model
   to confidently predict positive for any class.

4. AUPRC MAINTAINED (0.548) — RANKING IS OK, THRESHOLD IS THE PROBLEM:
   The AUPRC of 0.548 is reasonable (above per-class baselines) and
   close to the F1 baseline. The AUPRC is threshold-independent, meaning
   the model's probability ordering is decent — it's just calibrated
   conservatively by the noise. With a lower threshold (e.g., 0.35),
   F1 might recover closer to baseline.

KEY PROJECT CONCLUSIONS:
  1. GRL Speaker Disentanglement (F3) is the optimal architecture
  2. HuBERT-large layer 21 is the optimal SSL backbone
  3. PCA-32 is optimal dimensionality reduction
  4. Graph methods (E2-GAT) are strong for relational reasoning
  5. Augmentation at feature level is generally harmful
  6. ICA is the worst reduction method (statistical independence ≠ discriminability)

Key Hypotheses and Expected Outcomes
------------------------------------
- H1: Layer optimality is stutter-type dependent: Blocks (motor planning failure)
  may be better captured in later layers encoding prosodic structure, while sound
  repetitions (acoustic-phonetic events) may peak in mid-layers. Expected: per-type
  optimal layers differ by 3 to 8 layers.
- H2: Dimensionality reduction improves generalization: PCA to about 32 dimensions
  from 1024 should improve macro-F1 on minority stutter types by removing noise
  dimensions, similar to the 32-dim result in the dysarthria paper.
- H3: Handcrafted stutter features provide complementary signal: Features like
  jitter, pause ratio, and speaking rate should show low correlation with compact
  SSL+MFCC fusion (similar to <0.1 mean correlation in the dysarthria paper),
  indicating additive value.
- H4: Graph-based models outperform CNN on temporal stutter patterns: Disfluencies
  span multiple frames and involve non-local acoustic dependencies (for example,
  coarticulation changes before a block). GCN/D-GAT are expected to capture these
  better than frame-averaged CNN classifiers.
- H5: Multi-label formulation improves co-occurrence handling: Converting to
  multi-label binary classification should improve F1 on clips with two or more
  simultaneous disfluency annotations, expected to be a significant portion of
  SEP-28k.
