=====================================================================
WHY MFCC+SSL FUSION WORKS SO WELL
=====================================================================

1. COMPLEMENTARY FEATURE SPACES:
   HuBERT (1024-dim, L21): Deep self-supervised acoustic representations.
   Learns phonetic patterns, temporal structures, and acoustic correlates.
   Does NOT explicitly encode: fundamental frequency, spectral envelope,
   prosody, energy dynamics.

   MFCC-78 (39 + deltas): Classical spectral statistics.
   Encodes: mel-frequency spectral envelope (F0 proxy), rate of spectral
   change (delta), acceleration of change (delta-delta). Directly captures
   the acoustic signatures of:
   - Block: F0 drops to silence, energy drops sharply
   - Prolongation: sustained mel energy in specific bands
   - Interjection: distinct spectral envelope of "um/uh" fillers

2. TDNN DILATION CAPTURES MULTI-SCALE INTERACTIONS:
   Dilations [1,1,2,2,1] mean layer 3 sees features 2 steps apart in
   pseudo-sequence. Since the pseudo-sequence encodes sub-spaces of the
   fused representation, dilation effectively learns 2nd-order interactions
   between non-adjacent feature regions.

3. STATS POOLING (MEAN + STD) IS KEY:
   Standard mean pooling loses information about feature variability.
   StatsPool concatenates mean AND standard deviation:
   - Mean: "what acoustic properties are present?"
   - Std: "how much do these properties vary across sub-groups?"
   For stutter types, variability is diagnostic:
   - Block: high E(std) due to abrupt onset/offset
   - Prolongation: low std (sustained, steady)

4. BALANCING VS. SPECIALISATION:
   D6 achieves the most balanced per-class performance in the project.
   No class below F1=0.544. Precision≈Recall for Block (0.598/0.606).
   This suggests MFCC+TDNN avoids specialising on easy classes and
   instead treats each class more uniformly.

5. OVERFITTING PATTERN (best ep4, train→0.0015 by ep50):
   Same pattern as C4 and D3 — models with capacity ~1M params overfit
   rapidly on 20K samples. With early stopping at ep4-5, the checkpoint
   represents optimal generalisation. With proper regularisation (dropout=0.5,
   weight decay=1e-3, label smoothing=0.1), expected F1 ~0.62-0.64.

Key Hypotheses and Expected Outcomes
------------------------------------
- H1: Layer optimality is stutter-type dependent: Blocks (motor planning failure)
   may be better captured in later layers encoding prosodic structure, while sound
   repetitions (acoustic-phonetic events) may peak in mid-layers. Expected: per-type
   optimal layers differ by 3 to 8 layers.
- H2: Dimensionality reduction improves generalization: PCA to about 32 dimensions
   from 1024 should improve macro-F1 on minority stutter types by removing noise
   dimensions, similar to the 32-dim result in the dysarthria paper.
- H3: Handcrafted stutter features provide complementary signal: Features like
   jitter, pause ratio, and speaking rate should show low correlation with compact
   SSL+MFCC fusion (similar to <0.1 mean correlation in the dysarthria paper),
   indicating additive value.
- H4: Graph-based models outperform CNN on temporal stutter patterns: Disfluencies
   span multiple frames and involve non-local acoustic dependencies (for example,
   coarticulation changes before a block). GCN/D-GAT are expected to capture these
   better than frame-averaged CNN classifiers.
- H5: Multi-label formulation improves co-occurrence handling: Converting to
   multi-label binary classification should improve F1 on clips with two or more
   simultaneous disfluency annotations, expected to be a significant portion of
   SEP-28k.
