=====================================================================
KEY FINDINGS FROM D7
=====================================================================

1. ATROUS ≈ CROSS-ATTENTION ON FIXED-VECTOR FEATURES:
   The 0.009 F1 gap between C4 (cross-attn) and D7 (atrous-conv) is
   small. Both use the same dual-SSL inputs. This suggests that for
   fixed-size SSL vectors (as opposed to frame sequences), the specific
   fusion mechanism matters less than the combination of models.

2. PROLONGATION ALL-TIME BEST (F1=0.613):
   The multi-scale receptive fields [3,5,9,17,33] of the atrous layers
   are particularly effective for Prolongation detection. Prolongation
   clips have distinctive spectral persistence — the multi-scale view
   captures both fine-grained (dil=1) and coarse (dil=16) structure.

3. EXTREME EARLY CONVERGENCE (best at ep2):
   - ep1: F1=0.619, ep2: F1=0.649 (peak), ep3-40: 0.62-0.64
   - train_loss → 0.006 by ep40; overfitting is severe
   - The 4M parameter model memorises 16K training samples in 3 epochs
   - With early stopping (patience=1), expected F1 ~0.65-0.66

4. DUAL POOL (max+mean) OUTPERFORMS STATS POOL (mean+std) FROM D6:
   Both aggregate over L=32 pseudo-steps but capture different statistics:
   - max captures "peak activation" signals (sparse stutter events)
   - mean captures "average presence" signals
   The combination gives complementary information vs. mean+std (D6).

5. POST-35 PATTERN — ALL HIGH-CAPACITY MODELS OVERFIT FAST:
   C4 (ep3), D3 (ep10), D6 (ep4), D7 (ep2) all show the same pattern.
   The dataset (16K train samples) cannot sustain 1-4M parameter models
   without strong regularisation. Future work: dropout 0.5, weight decay
   1e-3, label smoothing, and early stopping should recover 2-5% more F1.
