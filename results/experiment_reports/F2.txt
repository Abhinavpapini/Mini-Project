=====================================================================
ANALYSIS
=====================================================================

1. MIXUP HURTS PROLONGATION (F1: ~0.47 → 0.287):
   Prolongation clips (elongated vowels) are created by interpolating with
   non-Prolongation clips. The resulting synthetic samples are acoustically
   ambiguous — partial vowel elongation that doesn't match real stutter patterns.
   With only 30.5% positive rate, mixed samples often produce pseudo-negatives
   (low λ × Prolongation feature + high (1-λ) × non-stutter). The classifier
   learns to be conservative → recall collapses to 17.8%.

2. MIXUP HELPS WORDREP (Recall: ~0.26 → 0.547):
   WordRep has the lowest prevalence (18.2%). Mixup creates synthetic WordRep-
   positive samples by interpolating with other stutter types, effectively
   upsampling with context. This dramatically improves recall (0.547 > all
   prior experiments) but at cost of precision (0.343 — many false positives).

3. TRAINING INSTABILITY (best at ep17, then flat 0.38-0.43 over 33 epochs):
   Mixup soft labels make the gradient signal noisier than hard labels.
   Combined with DimMask, the effective training signal is quite weak.
   The model finds a locally good solution at ep17 but can't improve further
   due to the augmented training distribution mismatch.

4. DIM-MASK MAY BE TOO AGGRESSIVE FOR 32-DIM FEATURES:
   Masking 20% of 32 dimensions = masking ~6-7 dims per sample. For a 32-dim
   PCA space where each dim is principal component, losing 6 dims means
   losing 6/32 = 18.75% of total variance. This is very significant.
   A smaller mask_ratio (0.05-0.10) might work better.
