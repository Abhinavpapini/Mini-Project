=====================================================================
KEY INTERPRETATIONS
=====================================================================

1. INTERJECTION IS EASIEST (F1=0.716): Best precision-recall balance (0.710/0.722).
   Interjections (um, uh, like) are highly linguistically stereotyped — Whisper
   encodes these as near-fixed tokens, making them easy to discriminate. Also
   Whisper is (very slightly) dominant for this target, supporting the linguistic
   explanation.

2. WORD REPETITION IS HARDEST (F1=0.568): Low class balance (18.15% pos rate) and
   low precision (0.482) — many false positives. Word-level repetition spans are
   acoustically similar to normal speech and hard to separate from both acoustic
   and linguistic features alone. May need full temporal sequence modeling (D1/D4).

3. HIGH RECALL ACROSS ALL TARGETS (avg 71.2%): The class-weighted loss successfully
   pushes the model toward detecting actual stutter events, at some cost to precision.
   For clinical screening (missing a stutter = bad), high recall is desirable.

4. BEST EPOCH IS EARLY (avg ~10.6): Val F1 plateaus quickly then barely improves.
   This confirms that PCA-32 is an information bottleneck. Future improvement:
   try PCA-64 or PCA-128 for C5 variant, or use raw embeddings with stronger
   regularization.

5. ACCURACY >> F1 ON MINORITY TARGETS: WordRep accuracy=80.9% but F1=56.8%.
   This gap grows as class imbalance increases, confirming the need for F1 as
   primary metric (not accuracy) for SEP-28k evaluation.

Key Hypotheses and Expected Outcomes
------------------------------------
- H1: Layer optimality is stutter-type dependent: Blocks (motor planning failure)
   may be better captured in later layers encoding prosodic structure, while sound
   repetitions (acoustic-phonetic events) may peak in mid-layers. Expected: per-type
   optimal layers differ by 3 to 8 layers.
- H2: Dimensionality reduction improves generalization: PCA to about 32 dimensions
   from 1024 should improve macro-F1 on minority stutter types by removing noise
   dimensions, similar to the 32-dim result in the dysarthria paper.
- H3: Handcrafted stutter features provide complementary signal: Features like
   jitter, pause ratio, and speaking rate should show low correlation with compact
   SSL+MFCC fusion (similar to <0.1 mean correlation in the dysarthria paper),
   indicating additive value.
- H4: Graph-based models outperform CNN on temporal stutter patterns: Disfluencies
   span multiple frames and involve non-local acoustic dependencies (for example,
   coarticulation changes before a block). GCN/D-GAT are expected to capture these
   better than frame-averaged CNN classifiers.
- H5: Multi-label formulation improves co-occurrence handling: Converting to
   multi-label binary classification should improve F1 on clips with two or more
   simultaneous disfluency annotations, expected to be a significant portion of
   SEP-28k.
