=====================================================================
WHY AE FAILS WORSE THAN KPCA AND MDS
=====================================================================

1. MSE RECONSTRUCTION ≠ STUTTER CLASS DISCRIMINABILITY:
   The AE bottleneck learns to RECONSTRUCT 1024-dim HuBERT features.
   But discriminating between stutter types doesn't require reconstructing
   all 1024 dimensions — it requires preserving specific spectro-temporal
   patterns. The MSE loss has no concept of stutter types.

2. INTERJECTION COLLAPSE (F1=0.359 vs usual 0.60):
   Interjection clips ("um", "uh") have very typical short-duration acoustic
   patterns. The AE may compress these into a specific bottleneck region
   BUT the classifier never sees a clean separation because the MFCC-like
   low-level variability (background noise, speaker variation) is also
   compressed into the same 32 dims.

3. AE NOT CONVERGED AT 50 EPOCHS (loss=0.471, still declining):
   The AE recon loss drops every 10 epochs: 0.526→0.493→0.481→0.474→0.471
   The bottleneck features at ep50 are still noisy. More AE epochs (100-200)
   would improve reconstruction but not necessarily classification.

4. 32-DIM IS TOO SMALL FOR RECONSTRUCTION OF 1024-DIM SSL FEATURES:
   Compressing HuBERT-1024 to 32-dim (32× compression) via reconstruction
   forces the AE to discard most acoustic detail. PCA-32 at least retains
   the top-variance directions which happen to be task-relevant.
   AE-32 retains reconstruction-optimal directions which are NOT necessarily
   the classification-optimal directions.

5. BOTTLENECK FEATURES TOO DIFFERENT FROM PCA STRUCTURE:
   PCA creates orthogonal components with guaranteed maximum variance.
   AE creates entangled, non-orthogonal components optimised for MSE.
   The CNN-1D architecture (designed for PCA-like structured features)
   performs worse on AE features.

=====================================================================
B-SERIES CONCLUSION — FINAL FINDING
=====================================================================

⭐ DEFINITIVE B-SERIES FINDING:
   For deep SSL features (HuBERT-large), LINEAR PCA IS THE OPTIMAL
   DIMENSIONALITY REDUCTION METHOD.

   All tested alternatives:
   - KPCA-RBF (B2): -0.10 F1 vs PCA
   - MDS (B3)      : -0.03 F1 vs PCA  (best alternative, moderately harmful)
   - AE (B4)       : -0.11 F1 vs PCA  (worst alternative)

   Why: HuBERT's 24 Transformer layers already perform sophisticated
   non-linear feature extraction. The resulting feature space is
   approximately linear at the macro level — PCA efficiently extracts
   the most discriminative directions. Any additional non-linear or learned
   compression introduces information loss without compensating gains.

   RECOMMENDATION: Use PCA-32 to PCA-64 for HuBERT feature compression.
   Do not use KPCA, MDS, AE, or VAE as intermediate compression steps.

Key Hypotheses and Expected Outcomes
------------------------------------
- H1: Layer optimality is stutter-type dependent: Blocks (motor planning failure)
   may be better captured in later layers encoding prosodic structure, while sound
   repetitions (acoustic-phonetic events) may peak in mid-layers. Expected: per-type
   optimal layers differ by 3 to 8 layers.
- H2: Dimensionality reduction improves generalization: PCA to about 32 dimensions
   from 1024 should improve macro-F1 on minority stutter types by removing noise
   dimensions, similar to the 32-dim result in the dysarthria paper.
- H3: Handcrafted stutter features provide complementary signal: Features like
   jitter, pause ratio, and speaking rate should show low correlation with compact
   SSL+MFCC fusion (similar to <0.1 mean correlation in the dysarthria paper),
   indicating additive value.
- H4: Graph-based models outperform CNN on temporal stutter patterns: Disfluencies
   span multiple frames and involve non-local acoustic dependencies (for example,
   coarticulation changes before a block). GCN/D-GAT are expected to capture these
   better than frame-averaged CNN classifiers.
- H5: Multi-label formulation improves co-occurrence handling: Converting to
   multi-label binary classification should improve F1 on clips with two or more
   simultaneous disfluency annotations, expected to be a significant portion of
   SEP-28k.
