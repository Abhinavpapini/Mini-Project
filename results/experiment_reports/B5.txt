=====================================================================
WHY VAE BEATS AE (B5 > B4 by +0.095 F1)
=====================================================================

1. KL REGULARISATION → STRUCTURED LATENT SPACE:
   VAE's KL(q||p) ≡ N(0,1) prior incentivises each latent dim to be
   independent and carry distinct information. This disentanglement makes
   it easier for the CNN-1D to selectively activate on stutter-type-relevant
   dimensions. AE has no such constraint → entangled, noisy dimensions.

2. β ANNEALING PREVENTS POSTERIOR COLLAPSE:
   Without annealing, β=0.5 from epoch 1 → KL forces all μ→0 before
   the decoder learns meaningful mappings (posterior collapse). The warmup
   (β=0 → 0.5 over 20 epochs) allows the encoder to first learn useful
   reconstructions, then gradually regularise. KL stabilised at 0.226
   (not zero → no collapse).

3. PROBABILISTIC BOTTLENECK = BETTER GENERALISATION:
   The stochastic training (z = μ + σε) acts as data augmentation in
   latent space — each forward pass applies small perturbations around μ.
   This prevents the encoder from overfitting to training noise, resulting
   in latent representations that generalise better to test clips.

4. INTERJECTION RECOVERY (F1=0.573 vs B4's 0.359):
   B4's deterministic AE collapsed Interjection patterns. VAE's structured
   prior maintains these in distinct latent regions. Interjection is now
   the highest-performing class again (as in most other experiments).
