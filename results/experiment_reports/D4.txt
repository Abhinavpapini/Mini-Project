=====================================================================
ROOT CAUSE ANALYSIS
=====================================================================

Why does the pure Transformer fail so badly on 32-dim PCA features?

1. LOSS OF LOCAL INDUCTIVE BIAS:
   The depthwise convolution in D1's ConformerBlock acts as a LOCAL filter
   over adjacent PCA feature positions. With 32 PCA components ordered by
   variance, nearby components share variance structure. The conv layer
   captures this local co-variation between adjacent principal components.
   Without it, the model sees each component in isolation via global attention.

2. PURE MHSA IS PERMUTATION-INVARIANT:
   D4's Transformer treats the 32 PCA tokens as a permutation-invariant set
   (no positional encoding). Since the 32 PCA dimensions ARE ordered (by
   decreasing explained variance), this ordering information is discarded.
   D1's conv uses kernel position implicitly, preserving this ordering.

3. EXTREMELY LOW RECALL (0.212):
   D4 produces high-precision but low-recall predictions. With sampler turning
   off, the model defaults to predicting negatives — it learns the "easy" signal
   (majority negative class) but cannot capture variety across stutter types.

4. PROLONGATION AND INTERJECTION COLLAPSE (F1 = 0.08 and 0.16):
   These two classes may have more distributed / subtle phonetic signatures
   that require local feature-space smoothing (conv) to detect. Pure MHSA
   cannot localise these patterns in PCA space.

5. IMPLICATION: THE CONFORMER'S CONV IS THE KEY COMPONENT:
   For 32-dim PCA feature space treated as a token sequence, the depthwise
   conv performs essential local smoothing. This is the mechanism responsible
   for D1 beating D4 by +0.276 Macro-F1.

CONCLUSION — ABLATION VERDICT
=====================================================================

❌ D4 FAILS: Macro-F1=0.272 (worst multi-label result across all experiments)

✅ D1 ABLATION WINNER: D4 conclusively demonstrates that the depthwise
   convolution in D1's Conformer is the CRITICAL component — not the
   multi-head attention. Removing conv loses 50% of macro-F1.

Practical consequence: For future deep model design with pooled feature
inputs (PCA-reduced), always include a local convolution module. Pure
Transformer attention over compressed feature vectors does not generalise.

D4 is retained for documentation purposes (important negative result)
and will NOT be promoted to the final model shortlist.

Key Hypotheses and Expected Outcomes
------------------------------------
- H1: Layer optimality is stutter-type dependent: Blocks (motor planning failure)
   may be better captured in later layers encoding prosodic structure, while sound
   repetitions (acoustic-phonetic events) may peak in mid-layers. Expected: per-type
   optimal layers differ by 3 to 8 layers.
- H2: Dimensionality reduction improves generalization: PCA to about 32 dimensions
   from 1024 should improve macro-F1 on minority stutter types by removing noise
   dimensions, similar to the 32-dim result in the dysarthria paper.
- H3: Handcrafted stutter features provide complementary signal: Features like
   jitter, pause ratio, and speaking rate should show low correlation with compact
   SSL+MFCC fusion (similar to <0.1 mean correlation in the dysarthria paper),
   indicating additive value.
- H4: Graph-based models outperform CNN on temporal stutter patterns: Disfluencies
   span multiple frames and involve non-local acoustic dependencies (for example,
   coarticulation changes before a block). GCN/D-GAT are expected to capture these
   better than frame-averaged CNN classifiers.
- H5: Multi-label formulation improves co-occurrence handling: Converting to
   multi-label binary classification should improve F1 on clips with two or more
   simultaneous disfluency annotations, expected to be a significant portion of
   SEP-28k.
